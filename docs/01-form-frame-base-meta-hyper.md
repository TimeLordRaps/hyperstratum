# Form, Frame, Base, Meta, Hyper

## Form

A form is an identifiable structure.

A form may be simple, local, partial, open, closed, or recursively composed. In Hyperstratum, form is the most general word for an identifiable structure that can appear, be interpreted, transform, relate, or close into a whole.

## Frame

A frame is an opening for interpretation, derivation, or transformation.

A frame does not merely contain a form. It gives conditions under which a form can be read, moved, transformed, or made meaningful.

```txt
frame -> allows movement
form -> identifiable structure
```

A frame can disclose a form. A form can become a component inside a larger frame.

## Base

Base is the local appearance or object-level form.

Examples:

```txt
word
claim
gesture
body-state
image
law
song
model-output
institutional role
```

The base is what appears before its recursive conditions are unfolded.

## Meta

Meta is the representation, interpretation, constraint, transformation, or condition of the base.

Examples:

```txt
A word's meaning.
A claim's intent.
A gesture's social reading.
A body's state interpretation.
A theorem's proof context.
A model-output's evaluation frame.
```

Meta does not mean detached abstraction. It means a form's condition, interpretation, representation, or transformation layer.

## Hyper

Hyper is recursive meta-connective structure across base/meta/whole-form levels.

A hyper-object is not merely bigger or more abstract. Its identity includes:

```txt
base appearance
meta-interpretations
recursive transformations
cross-level connections
whole-form role
closure-pressure
```

Compact:

```txt
hyper = base/meta recursion + cross-level connection + whole-form closure-pressure
```

## Dependency

```txt
base = local form
meta = condition or representation of local form
hyper = recursive connection across base/meta/meta-meta levels toward whole-form closure
```
